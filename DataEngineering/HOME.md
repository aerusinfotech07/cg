## What Is Data Engineering?

**Data engineering** is the discipline of **designing, building, and operating** systems that collect, store, process, and deliver data so organizations can use it for analytics, machine learning, and operations. It focuses on **pipelines**, **platforms**, **quality**, and **scale**.

---

## Why Learn Data Engineering?

| Benefit | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Career demand** | Data engineers are needed wherever data is central—tech, finance, healthcare, retail, and more. |
| **Foundation for analytics & ML** | Reliable data infrastructure enables dashboards, reports, and models. |
| **Problem-solving at scale** | You learn to handle volume, velocity, variety, and veracity of data. |
| **Transferable skills** | SQL, Python, cloud, and data modeling apply across roles. |

## Core Themes in This Tutorial Series

| Theme | What you learn |
|-------|----------------|
| **Introduction** | Definitions, lifecycle, roles, sources, and formats. |
| **Data collection** | Ingestion patterns, batch vs stream, APIs, CDC. |
| **Data storage** | Lakes, warehouses, databases, file formats, partitioning. |
| **Data processing** | ETL/ELT, transformations, batch and stream processing. |
| **Data integration** | Combining sources, schemas, master data, APIs. |
| **Quality & governance** | Rules, catalogs, lineage, stewardship, compliance frameworks. |
| **Security & privacy** | Encryption, access control, PII, regulations (e.g. GDPR awareness). |
| **Tools & technologies** | SQL, Python, Spark, orchestration, cloud services—overview and how they fit. |

## Who Should Learn?

- Aspiring **data engineers**, **analytics engineers**, and **platform engineers**  
- **Software developers** moving toward data-heavy systems  
- **Analysts** who want to understand how data reaches them  
- Anyone preparing for **certifications** or **interviews** in data infrastructure  

---

## Prerequisites

- Basic **programming** (Python or similar) helps.  
- **SQL** basics are important for storage and processing modules.  
- Comfort with **files, JSON, CSV**, and command-line tools.  
- No advanced math required for these introductory modules.
